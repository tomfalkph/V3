Manheim is one of those Lancaster County towns where old and brand-new sit side by side - long-established homes near the center and a wave of developments that have gone up over the last couple of decades. Both keep us busy, but the newer ones have brought a pattern worth flagging.

### Why so many newer systems need replacing

A lot of the homes around Manheim went up ten to fifteen years ago, and a fair number of them got builder-grade heating and cooling equipment. Several manufacturers in that segment, in that era, had some material and design flaws work their way into the units - and what we see now is that systems built in those years tend to make it only ten to fifteen years before they need replacing, rather than the twenty-plus you'd hope for. So if you're in one of those developments and a system that isn't really that old is already on its way out, there's a reason for it. When we replace one, we fit the new equipment properly to the home, so the second system does better than the first.

### Work around town

On the commercial side, we do work for the Lancaster-Lebanon IU13 school system, a good deal of it around Manheim - steady, get-it-right work we like. And the older homes in and around the borough bring the everyday plumbing and heating that comes with age.
