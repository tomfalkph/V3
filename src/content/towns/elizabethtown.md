We don't get out to Elizabethtown for every little thing - it's toward the western edge of where we range - but there's one job that keeps pulling us to Etown and neighboring Mount Joy: water conditioning. The water in this pocket of the county is genuinely hard. The last softener we put in around Elizabethtown was treating water testing at twenty-four grains of hardness, which is high by any measure.

### What water that hard does

At that level, hard water isn't a nuisance, it's a cost. It scales up the inside of pipes and water heaters, leaves fixtures crusted and glassware cloudy, and quietly shortens the life of every appliance the water touches. A softener sized and set to your actual water - tested, not guessed - turns that around, and it's some of the most appreciated work we do out this way.

### And the rest of it

Hard water is what we're known for around Elizabethtown and Mount Joy, but we're a full plumbing and heating shop - furnace and AC installs, water heaters, and everyday repairs - for the times a project's worth bringing us out for.
